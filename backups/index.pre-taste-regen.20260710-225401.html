01 / THE SEED

Curiosity became the seed.

Start from the real constraint: who fails, what breaks, what decision is blocked. Tools come second.

Proof · 20+ years of IT programs framed as problems, not feature lists.
02 / STRUCTURE

Engineering gave it structure.

Boundaries, flows, failure modes, feedback — and one practical question: does it actually work in production?

Proof · Full-stack delivery: design → build → operate, not slideware handoffs.
03 / LEVERAGE

AI gave it leverage.

Models search, draft, and challenge. People still own the decision. I design AI so review is natural — not a checkbox after the damage.

Proof · fmr: AI metrics only land after human confirm · agreyfox.github.io/fmr
04 / REASONING

Evidence keeps it honest.

Facts are not opinions. Logs beat narratives. Missing evidence is a finding — not a detail to skip.

Proof · Consulting habit: requirements and acceptance criteria written so they can be tested.
05 / STORIES

Incentives shape the system.

Data, incentives, and narrative collide. Good systems account for how people actually behave — not how a deck says they should.

Proof · Stakeholder workshops that map incentives before architecture freezes.
06 / THE COMPASS

Judgment stays human.

What should technology serve? Where must a person remain in the loop? I keep those questions next to the backlog — not in a separate ethics slide.

Proof · Product stance: no silent auto-commit of sensitive AI output (see fmr review flow).
